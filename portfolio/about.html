My parents tell the story of when I was less than a year old, and my father and sister were playing in the deep end of the pool. I was sitting on the steps, but I wanted to be with them, so I put my head down and paddled over. No one had taught me, I just did it. That has been my modus operandi ever since.

Being mildly autistic, I was slow to develop speech, so to communicate I would draw. I spent hours standing at the chalkboard, drawing, erasing, then drawing again. Later, using markers on paper, I would finish one drawing straight after another. It should come as no surprise that when I learned to create digital art, I would pursue it with the same dogged dedication and confidence – learning by doing.

In grade school, I would create crude video animatics. Years later, having discovered digital art speedpaint videos, I learned to reproduce anything that caught my eye. That then led me to pursue a degree in Graphic Design at Seminole State College, and now Game Design at University of Central Florida.

I have spent thousands of hours creating digital art and building my portfolio. I have learned how to compose an illustration utilizing perspective, color theory, conveying emotion in each character’s face, eyes, and body, thus telling their stories.

My skills include every app in Adobe Suite as well as Maya and ZBrush. I excel in vectorized art, raster illustration, photomanipulation, compositing, formatting and animation, as well as video creation and editing. I’ve even published a book of short stories with my own illustrated cover art!
